Your Sample Pre-Med Course Roadmap

A strong medical-track schedule should balance rigor and GPA. Colleges like to see advanced science, math, writing, and meaningful electives, but not at the cost of weak grades.

Priority 1

Protect your GPA. A slightly less intense schedule with stronger grades is better than overload with poor grades.

Priority 2

Take at least one major advanced science class: AP Biology, AP Chemistry, AP Physics, or Anatomy.

Priority 3

Pair courses with activities: research, volunteering, HOSA, public health projects, or clinical exposure.
