Personalized Resume Tips

  • Use numbers wherever possible: hours completed, people helped, funds raised, students mentored, or events organized.
  • Turn tasks into impact: do not just say what you did. Explain what changed because of your work.
  • Separate clinical exposure from general volunteering: this makes the resume easier for pre-med reviewers to read.
  • Add leadership details: founder, officer, coordinator, team lead, trainer, mentor, or organizer.
  • Keep descriptions short: each bullet should be clear, professional, and focused on action.
